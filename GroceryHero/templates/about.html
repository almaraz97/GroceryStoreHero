Recipe Harmony Tool FAQs
What is the Recipe Harmony Tool?

The Recipe Harmony Tool is a way of grouping recipes together that overlap in ingredients so that buying in bulk and preventing food waste is easier; saving you money and in a small way, helping the earth. It can evaluate anywhere from hundreds to millions of recipe combinations and recommend the top 3 that fit your specific preferences.

What is the meaning of the Recipe Harmony Score?

The harmony score is a way to measure how similar a group of recipes are by their shared ingredients. But that's not the only thing it can consider. By adding utilizing some of the advanced harmony settings in your account page the harmony score can consider which ingredients you do or do not want to see very often, which recipes you want or don't want together and more; all of which will affect the harmony score. After configuring these settings it may be possible to achieve a score higher than 100%.

What is a good Recipe Harmony Score?

Generally, anything lower than 20 means there are next to no ingredient overlap while anything above 70 means they are practically the same recipe. A score of 100% is possible but only for completely identical recipes. From my experience a group score higher than 50% tends to taste very similar but with advanced settings configured that can be avoided. By configuring those it may be possible to get a score higher than 100%!

What is the Harmony Mode setting in the advanced settings?

If you have 3 apples, 1 ground beef, 2 yogurts, and 4 sodas on your grocery list, this is how the modes would each interact.

  • Charity only wants to make the weakest link (ground beef in this case) as big as possible
  • Fairness wants every item to be the same quantity (increase ground beef, then yogurt then apples) and then increase them evenly
  • Balanced and Selfish want all items to get bigger but
  • Selfish with a higher priority towards the bigger items
  • Greedy only wants the largest quantity item (sodas in this case) as big as possible.

Other Tips to Being a Grocery Hero
  • Ingredients that are metric and ones that are Imperial will not combine in the grocery list as of currently. If you have 1 pound of chicken and 100 grams of chicken, they will stay separated.
  • When importing recipes or aisles, new aisles or recipes that share a name with an aisle or recipe you already have will not be added.
  • You can only add extras from aisles currently. Add them to their aisle so the extra item can be sorted when you add it to you list.
